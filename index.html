What Makes a Good Activity?

Understanding the three key pillars that make activities stand out to college admissions officers

Sustainability

Long-term commitment and consistent engagement over time. Colleges want to see dedication and growth through sustained involvement.

Examples:

  • Leading a club for 2+ years
  • Volunteering weekly for 6+ months
  • Building a project over multiple semesters
  • Maintaining relationships with community partners

Impact

Measurable positive change in your community or field. Show concrete results and meaningful outcomes from your efforts.

Examples:

  • Raising $10,000+ for a cause
  • Teaching 100+ students coding
  • Reducing waste by 50% in your school
  • Creating jobs for 20+ people

Originality

Unique approach, innovative solutions, or creative problem-solving. Stand out by doing something different or better than others.

Examples:

  • Creating a new app or technology
  • Starting an original nonprofit organization
  • Developing a unique research project
  • Inventing a new solution to a problem

The Perfect Activity Combines All Three

The most successful college applicants create activities that demonstrate sustained commitment, measurable impact, and innovative thinking. Our programs help you develop activities that excel in all three areas.
